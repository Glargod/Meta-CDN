# Meta-CDN | Systems Thinking Framework

## Meta Constraint-Drift-Narrative Framework

**Meta-CDN** (v1.2 — Consensus Distortion Edition) is an evolved analytical lens for truth-seeking in fragmented, high-uncertainty environments.

It helps separate **visible signals** from **underlying pressures**, **hidden realities**, and **steering forces** — while avoiding naive institutional trust or reflexive conspiracism.

### Consensus Distortion Principle
The thoughts of the few (mainstream institutions, gatekeepers, and concentrated power centers) disproportionately shape the perceived reality of the many. Conversely, the thoughts and observations of the many (independent, decentralized, alternative thinkers) are acknowledged or amplified by few.  

Consequently, **apparent consensus is inherently distorted**. True consensus emerges only when diverse thinkers converge with **minimal narrative distortion** (g(N) ≈ 1.0), prioritizing **falsifiable claims** over unfalsifiable ones.

## Core Equation (v1.2)

```math
P_effective = [D_measured × w_m + D_latent × w_l] × g(N) × S × O × I
```

Each factor follows **strict semantic contracts** for consistent analysis across users. v1.2 strengthens weighting of D_measured vs. D_latent under consensus distortion.

## The Six Layers

### 1. Constraints (C)
Structural inertia and path dependency — the resistant foundation of any system.

### 2. Drift (D) — Layered
Raw directional pressures. Apply **Consensus Distortion** lens when weighting:

- **D_measured** — Visible / mainstream signals (often amplified by the few)
- **D_latent** — Hidden or under-weighted forces (often from the many)

### 3. Narrative Field g(N)
Pure information distortion and amplification (neutral value = 1.0).  

**Key dynamic**: Mainstream signals (few-to-many) dominate perception while independent signals (many-to-few) remain under-amplified. Score g(N) deviations explicitly. Prioritize falsifiable evidence.

### 4. Shadow (S)
The yin/yang gap: overt appearance vs. hidden reality and baggage of actors.

### 5. Observer (O)
Explicit correction for the analyst’s own biases and blind spots.

### 6. Influence / Steering (I)
Who benefits from change? How effectively can they steer the reaction and solution phase?

## Key Features

- Strict semantic contracts reduce divergence between analysts
- Built-in observer bias correction
- Designed for longitudinal / trend tracking
- Requires explicit uncertainty bands
- Lightweight, portable, and domain-agnostic
- v1.2: Consensus Distortion analysis for evaluating mainstream vs. independent signals

## When to Use Meta-CDN

Especially powerful in **high-stakes, low-trust** situations where mainstream consensus is misleading or where **Consensus Distortion** is evident (few shaping the many while independent signals are marginalized).
